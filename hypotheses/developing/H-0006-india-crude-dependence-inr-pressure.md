## Statement
**Cause:** India imports ~85% of its crude oil. With oil at $106.59/bbl and INR at 96 vs USD, India's energy import bill is among the highest in its history relative to GDP. The Iran peace deal, which has temporarily suppressed crude, is on "mixed signals" and could collapse (or take 3–6 months to implement). Even if the deal closes, Iran's return to markets may be offset by OPEC+ production cuts. The structural reality: India has no domestic crude supply buffer, and any sustained crude above $100/bbl compresses India's macro headroom.

**Effect:** INR remains structurally weak in the 95–100 range (vs historical 80–86 range pre-2024). RBI stays in neutral/cautious mode, unable to cut rates aggressively. High crude = high inflation = RBI constrained = consumer loan rates stay elevated = urban consumption drag = companies dependent on consumer credit (autos, consumer durables, housing) face headwinds. FII currency drag reduces India EM attractiveness vs peers with stronger currencies.

**One-line:** India crude import dependence at $100+ oil + INR at 96 → RBI constrained from rate cuts → consumption drag → underperformance of credit-dependent sectors, over 6–18 months.

## Notes
**Linked to H-0002:** H-0002 (Iran deal → crude falls) is the resolution event for this thesis. If H-0002 confirms, H-0006 is retired. If H-0002 fails (deal collapses), H-0006 upgrades to active and becomes the dominant macro thesis for India.

**RBI's bind:** RBI cannot cut rates when crude is $100+ because: (a) high crude = high CPI (fuel component + transport costs across supply chain), (b) rate cuts further weaken INR, making imports more expensive. The RBI is structurally caught between growth support (wants to cut) and inflation/currency management (cannot cut). This is the thesis's core mechanism.

**INR at 96 in historical context:** Pre-2022, USD/INR was in the 72–76 range. The move to 96 reflects cumulative: US rate hike cycle, India CAD widening, crude price shocks. A return to 80–86 range requires either crude normalization (H-0002) or major FII inflow surge (which itself requires crude normalization or growth acceleration). Without either trigger, INR stays weak.
