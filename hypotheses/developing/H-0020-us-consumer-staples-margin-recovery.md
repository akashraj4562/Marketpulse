## Statement
**Cause:** Commodity input costs for US consumer staples (agricultural commodities, plastics, energy) have deflated 15–25% from 2022 peaks. Companies like Procter & Gamble, Unilever, Colgate-Palmolive, and Kellogg's (WK Kellogg) raised prices aggressively in 2022–2023 to protect margins. As commodity costs fall but retail prices remain sticky (consumers habituated to higher prices, limited retailer willingness to demand rollbacks), input cost savings flow directly to gross margin improvement.

**Effect:** US consumer staples companies deliver 150–300bps of gross margin expansion in FY2026–FY2027, driving earnings beats and re-rating from defensive-discount to quality-growth multiples.

**One-line:** Commodity deflation + sticky retail pricing → US staples gross margin +150–300bps → PG/CL/KHC EPS beats → defensive re-rating to 22–26x P/E.

```

  ↓ [Raw material COGS for staples companies falls; companies have ~6-month inventory buffer before cost saves flow through]

```

These are high-quality compounders temporarily de-rated — the re-entry window is the 3–4 quarter margin expansion cycle.
