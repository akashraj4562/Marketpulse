## Statement
**Cause:** India EV penetration in passenger vehicles is approaching 3–4% (FY26). Tata Motors leads (~60% EV market share), M&M is accelerating (BE6e, XEV 9e launch). The government's FAME III scheme and state subsidies are supporting demand. Charging infrastructure is expanding rapidly (Tata Power, ChargeZone, BPCL). Critically: Indian EV buyers are now overwhelmingly repeat purchasers (not just early adopters) — indicating demand is structural, not subsidized.

**Effect:** Tata Motors' EV segment achieves standalone profitability in FY27, triggering a sum-of-the-parts rerating. M&M's EV order book (200,000+ units) converts to revenue, turning the EV segment from cash-burn to contributor. Both stocks re-rate as the market moves from "EV story" to "EV proof."

**One-line:** India EV penetration hitting 5% tipping point → Tata Motors EV profitability → SOTP re-rating → Tata Motors +20–35% / M&M +15–25% over 2–4 quarters.
