## Statement
**Cause:** India's defense budget has grown to ₹6.2 lakh crore (FY26), with the government mandating ≥75% domestic procurement by 2026 under the Atmanirbhar Bharat push. The India-Pakistan tension in May 2026 has accelerated procurement decisions that were pending. The order pipeline for HAL (Tejas Mk1A — 83 jets, ~₹48,000Cr), BEL (electronic warfare suites, radar systems), and BDL (Akash, MRSAM, Pralay missiles) is the largest in India's post-independence history.

**Effect:** HAL, BEL, and BDL see 3–4 year revenue visibility that translates to sustained earnings upgrades as execution milestones are met. Institutional investors rerate from "government order uncertainty" to "defense industrial complex" multiples, driving P/E expansion alongside EPS growth.

**One-line:** India defense budget mandate + order pipeline acceleration → revenue visibility → EPS upgrades → HAL/BEL/BDL rerate from 25–30x to 35–45x P/E over 12–24 months.
