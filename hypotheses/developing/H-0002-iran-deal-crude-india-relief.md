## Notes
**The deal structure matters:** The Iran deal involves Hormuz reopening, not just a ceasefire. Iran's blockade of Hormuz is the supply mechanism; a peace deal without Hormuz reopening would have limited oil price impact. Watch specifically for language about Strait navigation, not just the war ending.

**Pakistan's mediator role:** Pakistan having skin in the game as mediator is a positive for deal credibility — this is not a Trump tweet, it's a structured diplomatic process. However, Pakistan itself has limited leverage to force Iran, so the process could extend.

**OPEC+ offset risk:** If Iran returns 2–3 million barrels/day to the market, OPEC+ (Saudi Arabia, UAE) may cut production to defend price. This is the main risk to the magnitude of the crude price decline — the $85–95/bbl range assumes partial OPEC+ offset. A fully unhedged supply return could push crude below $80.
