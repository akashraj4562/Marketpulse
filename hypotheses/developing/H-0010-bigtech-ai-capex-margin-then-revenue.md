## Statement
**Cause:** Amazon, Microsoft, Alphabet, and Meta are collectively committing $710B to AI infrastructure in 2026. Alphabet alone is spending $175–185B. This level of capex creates near-term margin compression (more depreciation, more data center opex) before the AI revenue monetization materializes. Apple, which has explicitly AVOIDED the AI capex arms race, is already demonstrating the alternative path — 18% EPS growth, 16% services revenue growth, without the capex burden.

**Effect:** (Phase 1, 1–2 quarters): Alphabet and Meta report operating margin compression as capex hits depreciation schedules. Stock prices face headwinds despite revenue growth. (Phase 2, 3–4 quarters out): AI-driven revenue products (Gemini API monetization, AI ad optimization, AI agents) begin accelerating revenue. The stocks recover and re-rate upward. Apple is the relative winner in Phase 1 because it has no capex drag.

**One-line:** Big Tech AI capex ($710B) → near-term margin compression → stocks range-bound 1–2 quarters → then AI revenue inflection lifts earnings → 2-phase trade.
