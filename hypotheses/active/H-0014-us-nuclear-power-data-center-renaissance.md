## Statement
**Cause:** AI data centers require reliable, 24/7 carbon-free power. Solar and wind cannot provide baseload. The US grid is straining — PJM interconnection queue has 3-year waits for new connections. Nuclear is the only proven 24/7 carbon-free baseload source. Microsoft signed a 20-year PPA with Constellation to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1 (branded "Crane Clean Energy Center"). Amazon, Google, and Meta are all pursuing similar nuclear PPAs. The US government extended the IRA nuclear PTC at $15/MWh.

**Effect:** Constellation Energy (CEG) and Vistra (VST) — the two largest US nuclear operators — command sustained premium power purchase agreement pricing (>$80/MWh vs. historical ~$30/MWh), driving 3–5 year earnings upgrades and continued re-rating.

**One-line:** AI capex → data center power demand → nuclear PPA surge → CEG/VST earnings supercycle → +40–70% over 2–4 quarters from current levels.

**Capital market thesis:**
Nuclear power is undergoing a secular repricing event that the utility sector has never seen. For 40 years, nuclear operators were penalized by regulators (post-Fukushima) and undercut by cheap natural gas. AI has inverted that dynamic in 18 months. A plant that was generating $30–40/MWh in spot markets is now under contract at $90–115/MWh for 20 years. The PV of that cash flow change is enormous — and it is still being modeled incorrectly by analysts using utility-sector frameworks. CEG is not a utility; it is a strategic infrastructure provider to the most capital-intensive sector in US history. The stock is expensive on historical utility metrics and cheap on the new cash flow reality. Each new hyperscaler deal signed is a hard data point. The thesis terminates when the existing US nuclear fleet is fully contracted — probably 2027–2028 — at which point the thesis transitions to SMR plays.
