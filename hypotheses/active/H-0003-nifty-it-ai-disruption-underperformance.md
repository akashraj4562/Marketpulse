## Statement
**Cause:** Generative AI is systematically displacing the labour-arbitrage model that India IT built its $315B revenue engine on — specifically automated coding, documentation, analytics, and code maintenance. This is causing: (a) FII selling of India IT on structural thesis concerns, (b) hiring stagnation at TCS/Infosys/Wipro/HCL (3,910 net adds in FY26 vs 600,000 in FY22), (c) earnings expectations being revised down, (d) multiple compression.

**Effect:** Nifty IT index underperforms Nifty 50 by an additional 5–10% over the next 2 quarters. Individual IT stocks (especially mid-tier service providers without AI pivot) continue re-rating lower.

**One-line:** AI disruption of labour-arbitrage IT model → Nifty IT underperforms Nifty 50 by 5–10%, over 2 quarters (by Q3 FY26).

**Capital market thesis:**
India IT sector faces a structural de-rating that the market has begun but not completed pricing. The 25% YTD decline is the market recognizing the problem; the next 2 quarters will be the market pricing the earnings impact. Historically, structural sector de-ratings overshoot on the way down before finding a new equilibrium (see US telecom 2000–2003, or India PSU banks 2015–2019). The current phase is mid-cycle de-rating. The trade: Nifty IT underperforms the broader index because (a) FII selling continues, (b) sell-side earnings revisions are negative, (c) the re-rating to a lower PE multiple is not yet complete. This is not a short thesis for absolute returns — Nifty IT may rise in absolute terms if the broad market rallies. The thesis is specifically about underperformance relative to Nifty 50.

**Relief bounce risk:** After a 25% decline, Nifty IT is technically oversold. The medium-term thesis would be temporarily defeated while remaining structurally intact. Distinguish between: (a) bear market rally (does not kill the hypothesis) and (b) genuine business model pivot success (kills the hypothesis).

**The GCC offset:** Global Capability Centers (1,900+ in India, 1.9M employees) are growing rapidly — but they are largely unlisted companies and their growth doesn't directly benefit listed Indian IT stocks. The listed IT stocks are the large outsourcers who are losing the GCC demand to in-sourcing. This is actually a negative for listed IT, not a positive.
